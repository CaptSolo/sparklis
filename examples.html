Example queries for Sparklis

This page lists a number of queries over different endpoints (mostly DBpedia) that can be built in Sparklis. For each example query, a link enables to directly jump to it in Sparklis, and to navigate further.

Example queries demonstrated in the YouTube video

  1. List of films directed by Tim Burton and starring somebody born after 1980, ordered by increasing birth date. Open in Sparklis
    give me a film
    • whose director is Tim Burton
    • and whose starring has the lowest-to-highest birth date after 1980
  2. List of film writers born in France with their English name, an optional depiction, their birth date, and their number of films as a writer (in decreasing order). Open in Sparklis
    • give me a writer
      • that is the writer of a film
      • and whose label has a language matching en
      • and whose depiction is an optional thing
      • and that has a birth date
      • and that has any birth place whose country is France
    • and for
      • each writer
      • and each label
      • and each depiction
      • and each birth date
      • give me the highest-to-lowest number of film

Example queries from the QALD-4 challenge (test questions from Task 1 on DBpedia)

  3. Which poet wrote the most books? Open in Sparklis
    • give me a person
      • whose occupation is Poet
      • and that is the author of a book
    • and for each person
      • give me the highest-to-lowest number of book
  4. How many programming languages are there? Open in Sparklis
    • give me a programming language
    • and give me the number of programming language
  5. Give me all Dutch parties. Open in Sparklis
    give me a political party whose country is Netherlands
  6. When was Carlo Giuliani shot? Open in Sparklis
    it is true that Death of Carlo Giuliani has a date of death
  7. Does the Isar flow into a lake? Open in Sparklis

    give me a river that is the inflow of a lake whose country is Germany
  9. How heavy is Jupiter's lightest moon? Open in Sparklis
    give me something
    • that is the satellite of Jupiter
    • and that has the lowest-to-highest mass
  10. Who is the youngest Darts player? Open in Sparklis
    give me a darts player that has the highest-to-lowest birth date
  11. Give me all animals that are extinct. Open in Sparklis

  13. Which ingredients do I need for carrot cake? Open in Sparklis

  14. What is the most frequent death cause? Open in Sparklis

  15. Who has Tom Cruise been married to? Open in Sparklis

  16. Who is the tallest player of the Atlanta Falcons? Open in Sparklis

Example queries about Nobel Prizes

  1. List of Peace Nobel prize laureates, in decreasing year. Open in Sparklis
    give me a laureate whose nobel prize
    • has as a category Peace
    • and has the highest-to-lowest year
  2. List of Nobel prize laureates, by decreasing number of Nobel prizes. Open in Sparklis
    • give me a laureate that has a nobel prize
    • and for each laureate
      • give me the highest-to-lowest number of nobel prize
  3. Number of Nobel prize laureates by gender. Open in Sparklis
    • give me a laureate that has a gender
    • and for each gender
      • give me the number of laureate

Example queries about geographical facts in the Mondial database

The Open in Sparklis links below include parameters to use English labels for classes and properties, and to use a Mondial-specific labelling property for entities. You may have to click the button after following the links to see the proper labels because they are retrieved asynchronously. Questions 7-10 illustrates the new capabilites of Sparklis to express computation expressions, nested aggregations, filtering expressions, and their combinations.
  1. Find rivers taking their source in Russia, and flowing into a lake. Open in Sparklis
    give me a river
    • whose source is located in Russia
    • and that flows into a lake
  2. Which capitals of which countries are on the border to the Atlantic Ocean? Open in Sparklis
    give me a city
    • that is the capital of a country
    • and located at Atlantic Ocean
  3. Give French cities with a population higher than 200,000 located along a river. Open in Sparklis
    give me a city
    • that is the city of France
    • and whose population is higher or equal to 200000
    • and located at a river
  4. In which mountain ranges do we find the highest number of volcanos? Open in Sparklis
    • give me a mountains that is the in mountains of a volcano
    • and for each mountains
      • give me the highest-to-lowest number of volcano
  5. Which languages are spoken in Spain? In which proportions? Open in Sparklis
    give me a spoken language
    • whose country is Spain
    • and that has a language
    • and that has the highest-to-lowest percentage
  6. Which religions are present in which countries at more than 5 percent? How many such countries are there for each religion, in decreasing order? Open in Sparklis
    • give me a belief
      • that has a religion
      • and that has a country
      • and whose percentage is higher or equal to 5
    • and for each religion
      • give me the highest-to-lowest number of country
  7. List countries by decreasing population density (=population/area). Open in Sparklis
    • give me a country
      • that has a population
      • and that has an area
    • and give me highest-to-lowest the population / the area
  8. Give me the total area of each archipelago, and the average total area across all archipelagos. Open in Sparklis
    • give me an archipelago that is the archipelago of an island that has an area
    • and for each archipelago
      • give me the total area
    • and give me the average total area
  9. Give me countries whose GDP per capita (=total GDP*1e6/population) is above the average. Open in Sparklis
    • give me a country
      • that has a total GDP
      • and that has a population
    • and give me highest-to-lowest the total GDP * 1e6 / the population
    • and give me the average value of the total GDP * 1e6 / the population
    • and where the value of the total GDP * 1e6 / the population > the average value of the total GDP * 1e6 / the population
  10. Give me the distribution of the number of islands among archipelagos, i.e. the number of archipelagos for each number of islands per archipelago. Open in Sparklis
    • give me an archipelago that is the archipelago of an island
    • and for each archipelago
      • give me the number of island
    • and for the lowest-to-highest number of island
      • give me the number of archipelago
